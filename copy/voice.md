Voice
•	Warm, incisive, slightly provocative (but never mean)
•	Smart, humanistic, story-forward
•	Short paragraphs. Occasional punchy one-liners.
•	Concrete images over buzzwords. Minimal exclamation points.

Core ideas to weave in
•	Most applications blur together because they optimize for “impressive,” not “radically personal.” Counterintuitively, it’s those who find meaning out of the seemingly mundane and ordinary who write the best essays.
•	Essays are proof of personhood: reflection, vulnerability, specificity
•	College as a social/intellectual ecosystem; peers matter as much as classes
•	Readers are humans with taste, fatigue, and a desire to meet someone real
•	Strategy = empathy for the reader + ruthless specificity

Constraints
•	Don’t invent stats, acceptance rates, or name-drop outcomes
•	Don’t sound elitist or “hack-y”
•	No cliché marketing (“unlock your potential,” “stand out from the crowd”)
